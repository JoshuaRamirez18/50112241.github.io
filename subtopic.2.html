Functional requirements to mitigate the Risk of Harm to Patients from Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare
Site Author
 Garcia Gomez, Juan M et at.


Abstract
 The study looks at seven primary risks that are associated with artificial intelligence in healthcare. They look at patient harm due to errors, misuse of tools, bias and inequities within the field. There is also a lack of transparency, privacy and security breaches, accountability gaps, and implementation obstacles. After the study, the authors propose fourteen functional requirements to mitigate these risks, which includes an A.I. password, user management controls, regulatory compliance checks, data-quality assessment, clinical checks, continuous performance evaluation, and bias checks. Their goal is to aim to align A.I. system development with the European Union regulatory rules and regulations to ensure safety and trust in the integration of A.I. in medicine.

 Artificial Intelligence for Clinical Decision Support in Sepsis
Site Author
 Du, Xianjin. Wu, Miao. Et al.
Abstract
 The article dives into the use of Artificial Intelligence in support with clinical decision-making for sepsis. Sepsis is a leading cause of death in critically ill patients. The research focuses on A.I. driven algorithms for early predictions, diagnosis, sub phenotyping, and prognosis assessment, and lastly the management of sepsis. The authors also highlight that machine learning and deep learning models will exhibit promising metrics and may outperform traditional scoring systems. However, the article highlights the significant challenges, including generalizability, workflow integration, and clinician acceptance. Ultimately, tools hold major potential, but they have to be deployed with caution and rigorous evaluation.
