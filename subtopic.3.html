Adressing AI Algorithmic Bias in Health Care
Site Author
 Ratwani RM, Sutton K, Galarraga JE.


Abstract
 As AI algorithms are becoming increasingly permeate within healthcare, including diagnostics to population-health management, it has to ensure fair and critical performance across all patients. The authors highlight how algorithmic bias may lead to harm, particularly when there are vulnerable groups who are under-represented or data proxies misalign with true clinical needs. As an example, they provide, cost‑based models under‑identifying Black patients despite higher illness burden in one case. The authors advocate for systemic processes to evaluate bias, incorporate diverse datasets, transparency, and ensure clinician oversight. As well as establishing monitored frameworks. In conclusion, bias mitigation is portrayed as not only a matter of justice, but also safety for patients.

 Ethical legal implications of AI-powered healthcare in critical perspective
Site Author
 Nasir, Mohammad. Et al.
Abstract
 The authors of this article critically investigate the ethical and legal challenges that have arisen from growing deployment of Artificial Intelligence systems in healthcare. From diagnostics to patient care and decision making. The authors are able to address and identify core issues including data governance, the erosion of traditional patient doctor visits, and emerging biases. They also look at privacy concerns for patients, access inequalities and cost barriers. They argue that the promise of A.I. must have a balance by frameworks that emphasize helps human clicnicans, and not replacing them. Ultimately, regulation must link human rights, equity, and accountability for the implementation of AI and design.
