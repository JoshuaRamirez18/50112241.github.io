The Role of Artificial Intelligence in Diagnostic Radiology
Site Author
 Strubchevska O, Kozyk M, Kozyk A, Strubchevska K.


Abstract
 This article dives into the impact of artificial intelligence (AI) on diagnostic radiology through analyzing eight defining studies published between 2018 through 2024. The authors assess A.I. and the diagnostic accuracy in interpreting radiological images (which includes: lung, mediastinal, and pancreatic disease) and evaluate its strengths and limitations in clinical practice. Results from the studies indicate that while A.I. shows promise like particular tasks, such as a chest-X-ray interpretation, it often requires human input. This is due to limitations in detecting more subtle findings. The authors conclude that AI has transformative potential for radiology workflow and its quality of service. However, further validation and integration into clinical practice would remain necessary.

 AI in diagnostic imaging: Revolutionizing accuracy and efficiency
Site Author
 Albadawy, Mona. Khalifa, Mohamed
Abstract
 The authors of this article are able to give a review of how artificial intelligence is essentially transforming diagnostic imaging by enhancing multiple factors. These include accuracy, speed, and clinical decision-making. The authors also examine the advances of deep learning for image acquisition, noise reduction, anomaly detection, and automated interpretation of modalities, such as a CT scan, MRI scan, and ultrasound. The article highlights A.I.'s ability to improve work efficiency, reducing human error, and supporting earlier diagnosis conditions like cancer or cardiovascular disease. The authors also touch on challenges related to data quality, algorithm transparency, and clinical integration. To sum up, the article demonstrates A.I.'s growing impact on modern imaging and the potential for improved patient diagnosis.
